Theorizing the "African City"

Conference

Is there such thing as an “African City”?

If so, what are its defining features? How does it stand alongside or apart from cities elsewhere? And if not, how do such cities “fit” into established urban frameworks?

In the first unit of our course, we consider these important questions related to the study of urban Africa, especially as it relates to understanding African cities alongside cities outside the African continent.

The writings featured here showcase students’ thinking about the above questions. Drawing upon wide-range of readings that detail how scholars, journalists, urban residents, and other observers have described and experienced African cities, those writings reflect students’ critical thinking about cities from diverse frameworks, perspectives, and points of view. They endeavor not to define an African city or to offer definitive claims about its confines. Instead, they offer thoughts on what students of history should do to try to “see” such cities on their own terms.

We invite you to read the pieces in this section as a means of accessing some of the critical and generative conversations we’ve had in the first few weeks of our course!
